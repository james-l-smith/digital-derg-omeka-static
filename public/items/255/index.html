Text

“A sudden flog of rain lashed into the windscreen. The sky had turned its bucket upside down. [Bobby] said: ‘Even if it’s raining do you still have to keep walking around those damn stones?’ ‘Yes.’ ‘You’ll get double pneumonia.’ ‘Don’t worry, darling. It’s called Saint Patrick’s Purgatory. He will look after me.’ That remark started a squabble that lasted until they drew up beside the lake. Other cars stood about like stranded boats. Other pilgrims stood by the boat slip, waiting for the ferry, their back hunched to the wind, their clothes ruffled like the fur of cattle. She looked out across the lough at the creeping works foam. He looked about him sullenly at the waiting pilgrims, a green bus, two taxi-loads of people waiting for the rain to stop. They were not his kind of people at all, and he said so.”
